Ek-Omkar

Ek-Omkar means "God is One" . The symbol is an emblem of the Sikh religion and is found on Gurdwaras (Sikh temples). Ek-Omkar is the beginning of the Sikh Mool mantra and the first phrase in the Sikh Holy book, the Guru Granth Sahib. Ek-Omkar is a combination of three letters: Ek - one, Om - the sacred letter for God, and the Kar - which is a line drawn over the Om, meaning the continuity, timelessness, eternal presence of Om (God) The Khanda is the emblem of the Sikh faith, symbolizing the four pillars of Sikh belief. It consists of four symbolic weapons.
